The whales of Kamay

Humpback whales embark on a remarkable seasonal migration... (Read more)

Humpback whales embark on a remarkable seasonal migration, spending their summers feeding in the nutrient-rich polar waters and their winters in tropical seas, where they socialize, mate, and give birth. Along the east coast of Australia, these majestic creatures can often be seen near Sydney. Pregnant and well-fed, humpback mothers typically appear in May and June as they travel northward, and they return with their calves from September to October, heading back to the icy waters of Antarctica.

The wild hucul of Hungary

The wild Hucul horses of the Aggtelek National Park... (Read more)

The wild Hucul horses of the Aggtelek National Park, Hungary, are a living link to a bygone era, embodying the rugged resilience of the Carpathian region. These hardy horses, often called "the little horse of the mountains," are direct descendants of ancient breeds used for work and transport in harsh mountain terrains.

Lady Musgrave Island

One of my favourite islands of the Great Barrier Reef... (Read more)

One of my favourite islands of the Great Barrier Reef is Lady Musgrave Island. Nestled within the Capricornia Cays National Park, Lady Musgrave Island is a breathtaking gem that captures the essence of marine and island beauty. Its (almost) fully enclosed lagoon, one of the few in the region, provides a natural safe haven for marine life.
